Electrical Traction

Traction is the action of drawing or pulling something over a surface, especially a road or track. So, electric traction is the system of traction involving the use of electricity.

Types of Electric Traction

Based on the supply system, electric traction can be broadly classi￿ed as; self contained traction and traction system fed from seperate distribution line. They are explained below:

Self contained traction

In this type, the vehicle itself has a provision for generation of electrical energy required for traction purpose. This include the following vehicles:

1.Diesel electric traction vehicles

2.Petrol elctric tracions vehicles

3.Battery driven vehicles

Generally, diesel electric traction is quite popular but nowdays, due to improvement in control and battery technologies, battery driven vehicles are being used widely for small scale traction purpose. In diesel generator, diesel engine is coupled to a dc generatio which feed the electric motors for producing necessary propelling torque. The generator used for this purpose may be self or separately excited. Since, the engine horsepower is constant and P ￿NT , torque produced is inversely proportional to speed i.e N￿T1 . The power obtained from the diesel engine is converted in to electrical power, which makes ease in controlling the output speed and torque for the proper traction operation.

Battery driven vehicles use the rechargeable batteries to operate motors for the traction. In some of traction system, due to some advantages, induction motors are used. For this, a inverter circuit is also provided to convert the battery dc power to ac power. To control the speed and torque of the motor, intelligent control systems are provided.They use various techinques of starting, speed control and braking of the electrical motors. Such as, voltage variation, ￿ux variation etc. But due to bulky nature of batteries and low e￿ciency these system are still not getting pace in operation.

Electric vehicles fed from separate distribution lines

The energy supplied fed tor traction system can be from separate distribution system. Special distribution feeder is provided for the traction purpose.Dedicated traction current lines are used when railways are supplied with low-frequency alternating current (AC). In many places use of traction power network (traction grid) are in use to provide separate supply network for traction services. The traction current supply line is connected to substations along the line of the railway and is usually run separately from the overhead catenary wire from which the locomotives are fed. The distribution system may be DC supply system or AC supply system.

1.Direct Current: DC traction units use direct current drawn form either a conductor rail or an overhead line. Generally, dc series motors are used for obtaining the necessary propelling torque, however, sometimes compound motors are also employed. The most popular line voltages for overhead wire supply systems are 1500 V DC and 3000V DC. The DC supply system is obtained by recti￿ying the ac voltages in substation. The AC supply is generally 33 kV or 66 kV and substation spacing is about 30 km to 40 km.

Dc supply systems have advantages of better characteristics of dc series motor, low maintenance cost. Also the weight of d.c motor per horsepower is less in comparison to ac motors. They do not interfere to the nearby communication lines too.

DC supply system requires additional substation cost for rectifying and boosting equipments. The dc equipments relatively draw more current requiring more conductor sizes and so cost is high.

2.Single Phase AC: In this system traction units draw alternating current form an overhead line. Typical

voltages used in this system are: 15 kV, AC 25Hz or 16.7Hz, 25 kV, 50Hz and 25 kV, 60Hz.The high voltages (15-25KV) obtained from overhead conductor are stepped down to a suitable motor operating range (typically 300-400V range) using step-down transformer carried by the locomotive unit itself.This single phase supply is fed to the locomotive unit via a single overhead line while track provides the return path.

3.Three Phase AC: In this, three phase induction motors are used for the movement of locomotive. This system normally works on 3000-3600V AC at a frequency either 16.7 Hz or normal 50 Hz supply frequency.

This system employs two overhead lines for two phases, whereas the track forms third phase. These conductors are powered from substations which are rated at higher voltages and they receive power from three-phase transmission lines.The high voltages from transmission lines are stepped down to 3.3 KV (3000-3600 V) by transformers while the frequency is reduced by frequency converters installed at substations.

The three phase induction motor used in this system has characteristics; simple and robust construc- tion, provision of regenerative braking without additional equipment and high operating e￿ciency, better performance, etc.

However, these motors have some drawbacks such as high starting current, low starting torque, complicated overhead structure, especially at crossings and junctions and not suitable constant speed characteristics of induction motor traction work.

Advantages of Electric Traction System

Some of the advantages of electric traction system are listed below:

1.High power to weight ratio than other forms of traction such as diesel or steam.

2.No exhaust fumes or carbon emissions.

3.Less noise pollution.

4.The maintenance cost of an electric locomotive very low as compared to other traction system.

5.The motor used in electric traction have higher starting torque, so high acceleration can be acheived.

6.The speed limit of electric motor is relatively high.

7.Electric equipment can withstand large temporary overloads.

8.Electric braking is superior than mechanical braking.

9.Regeneration is possible.

Tramways, Trolleys and Electric Trains

Tramways

Tramways are the substitute of load bus operated by 600 V DC from the single overhead conductor which is positive polarity and negative polarity is taken from the track rail. The supply is given from suitable points from the substation. Tramways consist of two driving axles so control is possible at both ends which make tramways operated in both directions. For two driving axles, two DC motors are used which are in series parallel control. Field weakening method is used in order to obtain high speed in the suburban area. For the control, two drum type of controlled is used at each end. In present days, the control has become more complex that causes sometime driver failures occur. The main disadvantages of tramways are the need of track which is costly and not appropriate in all the area. Regenerative braking is used in the hilly region to slow down the tramcars

and mechanical braking is used to stop because electrical braking is ine￿ective for stopping. Rheostatic and mechanical braking are used in normal operation. Electromechanical drums are used in mechanical braking. Magnetic braking is provided for proper retardation. Magnetic braking is in fact electromagnet which is attracted to the rail track which braking.

Type of Motors Used in Traction

Traction motors are expected to have both electrical and mechanical characteristics suiting the required service conditions of traction work.

1.Electrical characteristics:

(a)High starting torque

(b)Simple speed control

(c)Better commutation.

(d)Possibility of dynamic or regenerative braking.

(e)Capability of withstanding voltage ￿uctuations.

2.Mechanical characteristics:

(a)They must be robust and capable to withstand continuous vibrations.

(b)They must be totally enclosed type.

(c)They must be relatively small in dimension and minimum weight.

Di￿erent kind of electrical motors used for traction purpose are:

1.D.C series motors

2.D.C Separately excited motors

3.A.C series motors- single phase

4.Repulsion motors

5.Three phase induction motors

D.C motors were one of the widely used motors in traction in early days due to its high starting torque and capability for high torque overload. Series motors are also suitable for better sharing of loads between motors. But they cannot be controlled easily by semiconductor switches and there are lots of problems with regenerative and dynamic brakings of series motor.

Due to advancement in semiconductor converters, separately excited motor is now preferred over series motor due to independent control of armature and ￿eld. This results the speed torque characteristics of separately excited motor can be shaped to satisfy the traction requirements. In addition the regenerative and dynamic braking of a separately excited motor are fairly simple and e￿cient.

Compound motors have the advantage of both series and shunt motors. So they are also widely used for traction purpose.

Due to availability of reliable variable frequency semiconductor inverters, Squirrel cage induction motor are also used for traction. They have the advantage of Ruggedness, low maintenance, lower cost, weight and volume, and higher e￿ciency.

Starting, Braking and Speed Control of Traction Motors

Starting

The most important processes associated with controlled electric drives are starting, speed control, braking, and reversal of the direction of rotation. Each process plays a vital role in traction system because they are prime things for the electric motor.

An electric Motor is started by providing supply to the motor. Supply in the motor is used to develop the torque in the motor which rotates the motor. The supply network to which the electric motor is connected a￿ects the selection of the starting device. Starting is associated with the excessive currents. The starting currents will add to the motor heating that depends on upon the rms value and the frequency of starting current.

If the motor is started with direct supply then it withdraws the current above permissible limits which can melt down the windings of the motor which is undesirable conditions so while starting the motor, the starting current has to be brought down to the permissible limit to protect the motor. The di￿erent methods that start motor suitable without any harm on traction motors are as follows:

1. Direct on line starting or full- voltage starting

In this type of starting method, full line voltage is provided. This type of starting method is used by observing the size of the motor, the location of motor etc. Usually, DC motors up to 2 KW and synchronous motor of 5 KW are started using this type of starting method. This type of method is used in low rating motor because starting current of that type of motor is usually small to cause the great damage on the windings.

2. Reduced voltage starting

In this type of starting method, traction motors are started by supplying reduced voltage to the terminal of the motor. As we already know, the starting current is very high that can cause damage so current has to be minimized. The minimization in current in three phase induction motor can be achieved by stator starting, star-delta starting, stator reactor, and auto transformer starting. The reduced voltage is changed to the full voltage after the synchronization or after attaining required full speed. Auto transformer starting in three phase induction motor is an example of reduced voltage starting.

Braking

The breaking of the motor is as important as starting and control of the motor. After disconnecting the motor from supply, the motor ceases to rotate but due to its inertia, the motor takes su￿cient time to stop. If the motor is large and heavy then the stopping will be su￿ciently large. Traction locomotive has to be stopped at the required moment and at required position. To make the locomotive stop at speci￿ed position and at speci￿ed time, braking is used.

Braking can be applied by mechanically and electrically. In mechanical braking, the rotating kinetic energy is converted to the heat energy which rises the temperature of the brake and its near surrounding. In electrical braking, the rotating kinetic energy is converted to the electrical energy and dissipated in the resistance in the form of heat or returned to the supply.

Types of Braking:

1.Plugging or Counter current braking:

If the connected terminals are interchanged, then the motor tends to rotate in opposite direction. This may be not applied for all motors but traction motor follows this same theorem. Plugging braking occurs when the motor windings are connected in the reverse direction of rotation at a time when the armature rotation is still in the forward direction. This is simple type of braking system.

In DC series motor it has to ensure that when the terminal is interchanged, the current must ￿ow in opposite direction either in ￿eld winding or in armature winding but not in both. Plugging braking results high current ￿ow because the applied voltage and back emf generated will be in the same direction so to protect from the high ￿ow of current, resistance is inserted while interchanging the terminals position.

In three phase induction motor, plugging is achieved by interchanging either two windings out of three windings. Interchanging the two windings changes the direction of the rotating magnetic ￿eld that tends to rotate the motor in opposite direction which applies the brake on the rotor. During plugging type braking, the motor acts as a brake and absorb the kinetic energy from the still revolving load causing its speed to fall. In squirrel cage induction motor, whole energy is dissipated within the machine but in phase wound rotor, some part of the energy is dissipated in the external resistance added in the rotor circuit. During plugging, the slip is (2-s) so the relative speed is twice the normal speed.

2.Rheostatic or Dynamic braking: In this type of braking system, the supply is disconnected and external resistance is connected. The motor is operated as a generator so a decrease in speed generates kinetic energy. The kinetic energy of rotation is converted to electrical energy which is dissipated in the externally connected resistance.

In DC series motor, the supply is disconnected ￿rst and is connected to the braking resistance but while doing so, it is necessary that the connections of the ￿eld winding are changed over. If this is not done then ￿eld current will also be reversed so no braking will occur. The ￿eld current has to ￿ow in a same previous direction.

Dynamic braking in series DC motor can also be achieved by feeding the ￿eld winding from an independent source and connecting a resistance across the armature terminals.

Rheostatic braking of the induction motor is di￿erent from the rheostatic braking of DC motor. In an induction motor, the stator winding is disconnected from the AC supply and DC supply is given to produce the stationary DC ￿eld. The stator windings act as the DC ￿eld winding and the rotor windings act as the armature winding. The squirrel cage induction motor has formed the load itself but in wound rotor motor, external resistance is connected to provide the load.

3.Regenerative Braking:

In another method of braking, the energy of the rotating part is wasted. In regenerative braking, the kinetic energy is converted into the electrical energy. Part of that energy is supplied back to source and rest energy is dissipated in the form of heat.

Regenerative braking in case of Dc series motor is not simple because reversal of armature windings also reverses the ￿eld windings so negative torque can't be generated. Even if the ￿eld winding is also reversed at the same time, the back emf will be very small and current ￿owing in the ￿eld windings would be in the wrong direction that results in the short circuit of supply. Due to this problem, regenerative braking isn't applied directly but with some modi￿cation.

Regenerative braking is the inherent characteristics of the induction motor. The three phase induction motor operates as induction generator which runs at a speed above the synchronous speed and it returns the power back to supply. The motor is made to rotate above synchronous speed by these processes

Switching over to a low-frequency supply in frequency-controlled induction motors to reduce the speed. The downward motion of locomotives. Switching over to larger pole number operation from smaller one in multi-speed squirrel cage motors.

The disadvantage of this method is that braking is only possible in super synchronous speed. So it is used in reduced level.

Speed Control

The electric motor has to be performed at variable speed required for speci￿c work process. Speed has to be controlled on Traction system so that required speed can be achieved easily. Controlling speed of traction motor doesn't include the change in speed with the variation in load. The required speed of motor is achieved by acting accordingly on the rotor or the transmission media. Speed control is used to be done manually at early age but nowadays with automation facility, motor system itself controls its speed. The speed control requirement depends on the type of drive. Some drive may need very wide range of speed; some may require constant speed so speed is controlled according to the requirement. Constant torque drive and constant power drive are the term related to the speed. Constant torque drives are those drives whose shaft torque remains constant over a given speed change and constant power drives are those drives whose shaft power which is multiplication of shaft torque and shaft speed remains constant over a range of speed change. Speed-time curve for a traction system

The Speed time characteristics of traction system can be explained on the basis of speed-time and torque, power time characteristics which are drawn for travel between two consecutive station. The train is accelerated at the maximum permissible torque, giving constant maximum accleration. The power is increased linearly with speed. At time t1 the base speed and the allowable power is reached. Further accleration occur at constant power. Torque and acceleration decreases linearly with speed. At time t2 the drive torque equals the load torque and steady speed reached. From t2tot3 train runs at a constant speed and constand drive power. This duration is called free running. At t2 = 3 supply to the motor is truned o￿, reducing the drive torque to zero. Now the train coasts due to its own inertia. At a suitable time t4 brake is applied to stop the train at the next station. The area beneath the speed-time curve gives the distance covered. Thus, larger the area, greater will be the distance covered in a given time or lesser will be the time taken to cover the given distance.

An electric heater is an electrical device that converts electric current to heat. Electric Arc Heating

When a high voltage is applied across an air gap, the air in the gap gets ionised under the in￿uence of electrostatic forces and becomes conductin medium. Cur- rent ￿ows in form of a continuous spark, called as arc. This arc between the electrodes produces heat and has a temperature between 1,000 ￿C and 3,500 ￿C depending on the material of the electrodes used. The use of this principle is made in electric arc furnaces. The arc can basically be produced by two ways:

1.By striking the arc between the charge and electrode: In this method, the heat is directly conducted and taken by the charge. The furnaces operating on this principle are known as direct arc furnace. These furnaces are used for production and re￿ning of various grade of steel.

Figure 9: Electric Arc Heat- ing

principle are known as indirect arc furnaces. These types of furnaces are used for melting of non-ferrous metals such as brass, copper and zinc.

Induction Heating

To understand the principle of induction heating, consider an air core transformer. Now remove the secondary coil of that air core transformer and place a material to be heated in its place. Then, when you apply a high-frequency voltage to the

primary side the secondary side also undergoes a continuous change in magnetic ￿ux and eddy current start to ￿ow through it. This eddy current heats up the material. In the case of ferrous material, rapid magnetizing and demagnetizing of the material also takes place and this also causes it to heat corresponding to its area under B-H curve. This kind of heating is known as hysteresis loss. So, in induction heating, we utilize the heat of what we considered as a loss in other electric machines.

The induction heating process is used in metalworking industries, for processes

Infrared Heating

The electromagnetic radiation which has a frequency below (infra) that of red color is called infrared frequency. According to Wien's displacement law, the thermal radiation emitted by bodies near room temperature happens to be in the infrared region. Many organic molecules, strongly absorb the infrared and as a result, the object or even human body is heated up. Waves Waves

Microwave Heating

Microwaves are the electromagnetic waves that have higher wavelength than that of infrared waves. In the visible range, a lot of energy is dispersed rather than absorbed. But, microwave radiation does correspond to rotational modes of water molecules, as a result molecules strongly absorb the radiation and heat up.

Microwave heating is used in food processing, drying of paper, textiles, baking of bread, manufacturing plastics, processing of cement, timber etc.

This type of heating has become popular as it is clean, quicker and provides uniform as well as in-depth heating. The furnace used with microwave for the heating purpose is known as a microwave oven. Microwave oven could be household one or industrial and corresponds to its power rating. They consist of a microwave generator and the body of the oven (which in general is made up of material to re￿ect microwaves). The material to be heated is kept in a special place arranged in the body of the oven.
