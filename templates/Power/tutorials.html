1. Introduction

1.1 Introduction to discrete time control system.

Discrete tim control systems ar control systems in which one of more variable can change only ate discrete interval of time. These instants, are denoted by kT or $t_k$, may specify the times at which some physical measurements is performed of at which the memory of a digital computer is read out.

The signal used in discrete time control system are in teh form of sampled data or in digital form.

The discrete time system uses discrete time signals which have the amplitude for only a distince points in time but it can assume continouous range of values of amplitudes. If the amplitude takes continuous values for distint points in time, it is called sampled data signal while, if amplitude takes only quantized values, then it is called digital signal.

Discrete time systems can be described by difference equations agter the appropriate descretisation of continuous time signal. Deiscretisation basically mean as the conversion of S-domain to Z-domain.

1.2 Signal Sampling, quanitizing and coding.

A sequence ofvalues at discrete time points is obtained through the sampling of conntinous time signals. Samplin, in other words, also called descretisation. Whenever a control system involves a digital controlled, sampking is needed. For example, for a solar tracking device, a peroidic sampling of intensity of light along the panel is taken and appropriate contrl process is done.

Since, sampled data may contain continuous range of values and it is no desirable for a digital computer, So quantinaisaion is done which converts the sampled analog amplitude to a digital amplitude(represented by binary numbers). this digital signal is processed by digital computer. the process of representing a continuous or analg signal by a number of discrete states is called amplitude quanitsation.

Coding is the conversion of digital signal into set of binary numbers. The sample and hold circuit and analog to digital converter converts continuous time signal into a sequence of numerically coded binary words. This process is called as coding or encoding. Similaryly, the digital to analog conversion process is called decoding.

A sampler converts te analog signal output from multiplexer into a train of amplitude modulated pulses and hold circuit holds the value of ampled pulse signal over time. S/H circuit is an intergral part of A/D converter to produce a number that accuretly represent the input signal at sampling innstant. Specific digital valued is assainged to certain level by the A/D converter to the output of S/H and hold circuit.
